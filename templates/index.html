How It Works

⬡

U-Net Architecture

U-Net is an encoder-decoder CNN with skip connections that preserve spatial detail. The encoder downsamples the image to extract features; the decoder upsamples back to the original resolution, guided by skip connections that carry high-resolution information from each encoder stage. This enables precise pixel-level segmentation.

◈

Dice Coefficient

Measures the overlap between the predicted and ground-truth mask: (2|A∩B|) / (|A|+|B|). Ranges from 0 (no overlap) to 1 (perfect overlap). Unlike accuracy, Dice is robust to class imbalance — critical when the cancer region is much smaller than the background.

◇

IoU Score

Intersection over Union (Jaccard Index): |A∩B| / |A∪B|. More strict than Dice — it penalises false positives harder. The standard benchmark metric in medical image segmentation challenges (MICCAI). An IoU > 0.7 is generally considered clinically acceptable.

△

Segmentation vs Classification

Classification only answers "Is cancer present?" (yes/no). Segmentation answers "Where exactly is it?" by generating a pixel-level mask. This provides actionable spatial information for clinicians: tumour location, boundary, and size — indispensable for surgical planning and treatment assessment.
